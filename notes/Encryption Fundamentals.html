Encryption
Fundamentals

How the internet learned to keep a secret

Encryption converts readable information into ciphertext, which can only be accessed using the correct key. This process ensures data confidentiality, integrity, and trust across digital communications.

Principles of Cryptography

These principles define how data is protected, transformed, and verified. They establish the foundation for secure communication through confidentiality, integrity, authentication, and non-repudiation.

Core Components

Hash Functions, Ciphers, and Key Exchange Algorithms are essential to cryptographic systems.

  • Hash functions generate unique representations of data for verification.
  • Ciphers encrypt and decrypt information using mathematical operations.
  • Key exchange algorithms enable secure sharing of encryption keys between parties.

Cryptography in Internet Security

Modern internet communication relies on encryption to protect data in transit. Secure websites, online transactions, and private messages use cryptographic algorithms to ensure confidentiality and authenticity.

End-to-End vs. Transport Encryption

  • End-to-end encryption ensures that only communicating parties can read messages.
  • Transport encryption secures data only during transmission between systems.

Understanding these layers helps identify potential points of vulnerability.

Certificate Authorities and Digital Trust

Certificate Authorities (CAs), Trusted Root-CAs, and X.509 Certificates validate identities and issue digital certificates.

These entities create a chain of trust, ensuring that users can confirm the legitimacy of websites and services.
