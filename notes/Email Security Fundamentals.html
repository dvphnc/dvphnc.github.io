Email Security
Fundamentals

A quick guide to keeping your inbox safe

Key Pillars of Email Security

  • Confidentiality: Ensures that only authorized users can access the email content.
  • Integrity: Protects email messages from unauthorized modification or tampering.
  • Authentication: Verifies the true identity of the sender.
  • Non-repudiation: Prevents a sender from denying they sent an email.

Protecting Data with Encryption

Email encryption scrambles the content, ensuring only the intended recipient can read it — preventing unauthorized access to sensitive information (PII, financial data, etc.).

Major Encryption Standards

  • S/MIME (Secure/Multipurpose Internet Mail Extensions): A widely used standard, especially in enterprise environments, that uses digital certificates from trusted Certificate Authorities (CAs) for encryption and digital signatures.
  • OpenPGP (Pretty Good Privacy): An open-source standard for email security. It uses public-key cryptography and is often preferred by individuals and privacy-focused users due to its "Web of Trust" model.

Common Email Threats

  • Phishing: Deceptive emails designed to trick users into providing sensitive information.
  • Spear Phishing: A targeted phishing attack using personalized information to deceive a specific individual.
  • Email Spoofing: Forging email headers to make the message appear as if it came from a trusted source.
  • Business Email Compromise (BEC): Compromising or impersonating email accounts to initiate fraud.

Analyzing Email Authenticity

To detect spoofing and phishing, analysts examine email headers for authentication results:

  • SPF (Sender Policy Framework): Verifies the sender's IP address against the domain's authorized list.
  • DKIM (DomainKeys Identified Mail): Uses a cryptographic signature to verify the message's authenticity and ensure it hasn't been tampered with.
  • DMARC (Domain-based Message Authentication, Reporting, and Conformance): Provides policy for receiving servers on how to handle emails that fail SPF or DKIM checks.
