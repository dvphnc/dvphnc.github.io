What is Cybersecurity?

Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting systems, networks, and data from theft, damage, or unauthorized access — using people, processes, and technology together.

Red vs Blue Teams

AspectRed Team (Offensive)Blue Team (Defensive)

FocusExploit, social engineeringMonitoring, hardening
ToolsMetasploit, Cobalt StrikeSIEM, EDR, firewalls
OutcomeBreak in and prove riskStop the breach

Expanded Roles in IT Security

  • Vulnerability Assessor — Finds and prioritizes weaknesses
  • Penetration Tester — Simulates real attacks
  • Web App Pentester — OWASP Top 10, logic flaws, APIs
  • Red Team Operator — Full adversary simulation
  • SOC Analyst — 24/7 monitoring and triage
  • Threat Hunter — Hunts evil before it’s detected
  • DFIR Analyst — Investigates breaches, preserves evidence
  • Threat Intel Analyst — Studies real adversaries
  • Malware Reverse Engineer — Tears apart malicious code
  • Security Researcher — Discovers zero-days and publishes

What’s a SOC?

A Security Operations Center is the nerve center of defense — a team (and often a physical room) that watches, detects, analyzes, and responds to threats 24/7 using SIEM, EDR, and playbooks.

Three Pillars of Cybersecurity

  • People — The humans who think, decide, and act
  • Processes — Playbooks, policies, incident response flows
  • Technology — Tools that detect, block, and alert

All three must be strong. One weak pillar and the whole structure falls.
