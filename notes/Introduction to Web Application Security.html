The invisible shield behind every login, payment, and message

Web Application Security is the practice of protecting websites and online platforms from unauthorized access, misuse, or disruption. It shields the digital experiences we rely on — from logging into accounts to online transactions — ensuring that data stays safe and systems remain trustworthy.

A Look Back: The Evolution of the Web

ARPANET (1969): The Foundation

In 1969, the Advanced Research Projects Agency Network (ARPANET) was born — the first packet-switching network and the true ancestor of the internet, built for research and military communication.

The Old Web: 1989–1990s

In 1989, Tim Berners-Lee invented the World Wide Web at CERN. By 1991, the first website went live — static pages connected by hyperlinks. Security? Almost none. Trust was the default.

The Modern Web

Today’s web is alive, dynamic, and global. We shop, bank, date, govern, and fight on it. With great connectivity comes great responsibility — and an explosion of sophisticated attacks.

Why It Matters: The CIA Triad

  • Confidentiality: Prevents unauthorized eyes.
    Example: HTTPS encrypting your password
  • Integrity: Ensures nothing was tampered with.
    Example: Digital signatures on software updates
  • Availability: Keeps services online for real users.
    Example: DDoS protection
